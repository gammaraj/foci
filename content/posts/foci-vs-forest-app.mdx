Forest is one of the most popular focus apps, with millions of downloads and a charming gamification model. But is planting virtual trees the best way to stay focused? Let's compare Forest to Foci — a free alternative that takes a different approach to productivity.

## The Core Difference

**Forest** uses gamification: stay focused to grow virtual trees, break focus and your tree dies. It's designed to make focus feel rewarding through visual progress.

**Foci** uses integration: timer, tasks, ambient sounds, and progress tracking in one window. It's designed to eliminate the friction of switching between apps.

Both work. The question is which approach fits your workflow better.

## When Forest Works Better

Forest is a good choice if:

- **Gamification motivates you** — the visual reward of growing trees genuinely helps you resist distractions
- **You want social accountability** — Forest's shared planting features let you focus with friends
- **You only need a timer** — you manage tasks elsewhere and just want a focus countdown
- **You like the aesthetic** — Forest's design is genuinely beautiful

## When Foci Works Better

Foci is a better choice if:

- **You need task tracking** — you want to know what you're working on, not just that you're "focused"
- **You want ambient sounds built in** — brown noise, rain, and lo-fi without opening Spotify or YouTube
- **You don't want to pay** — Foci is completely free with no ads or premium tiers
- **You work on desktop** — Foci's web app works on any device without installing anything
- **You want detailed analytics** — session heatmaps, per-project time tracking, and daily/weekly trends

## The Task Tracking Gap

This is the biggest difference. Forest tells you that you focused for 25 minutes. Foci tells you that you focused for 25 minutes *on a specific task*, and tracks cumulative time across sessions.

For students, this means knowing exactly how much time you've spent studying each subject. For developers, it means logging hours per project without a separate time tracker. For freelancers, it means automatic time tracking for billing.

Forest assumes you already know what you're working on. Foci makes task selection part of the focus ritual — which research suggests improves intention and follow-through.

## The Ambient Sound Advantage

When you start a Forest session, you get... silence. Or you open Spotify in another tab. Or you search YouTube for "brown noise 10 hours."

Foci has ambient sounds built directly into the timer panel. Brown noise, rain, café ambiance, white noise — all generated locally in your browser (works offline), plus curated lo-fi and Indian classical music streams.

This isn't a minor convenience. It's one less decision, one less app, one less tab competing for your attention.

## Price Reality Check

Forest costs $3.99 on iOS. The Android version is free but shows ads unless you pay. Browser extensions require the mobile purchase.

Foci is free. No ads, no premium tier, no "pro" features locked behind a paywall. You can optionally create a free account to sync across devices, but the full app works without signing up.

For a single purchase, Forest is reasonable. But Foci offers more features at no cost — which matters if you're a student or just don't want another subscription.

## The Gamification Question

Forest's tree-growing mechanic is clever. Killing a virtual tree by picking up your phone creates a small emotional cost that some people find motivating.

But gamification has limits:

1. **Novelty wears off** — the dopamine hit from growing trees diminishes over time
2. **It can feel childish** — some professionals find the metaphor distracting
3. **It's extrinsic motivation** — you're focusing to grow trees, not because the work matters

Foci takes a different approach: **make focus effortless rather than rewarding**. One window, everything you need, no friction. The reward is the work itself — and the streak counter provides gentle accountability without gamification.

Neither approach is objectively better. It depends on what motivates you.
