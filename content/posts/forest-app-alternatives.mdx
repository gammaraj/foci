Forest is a popular focus app with a clever premise: stay focused to grow virtual trees, break focus and they die. It's charming, but it's not for everyone — and at $3.99 on iOS, it's not free.

Whether you want more features, a different approach, or just a free option, here are the best Forest alternatives in 2026.

## Why Look for Forest Alternatives?

People switch from Forest for a few common reasons:

- **No task tracking** — Forest times your focus but doesn't track what you're working on
- **No ambient sounds** — you still need Spotify or YouTube for focus music
- **Gamification fatigue** — the tree mechanic gets old after a while
- **Price** — $3.99 isn't much, but free is free
- **Limited timer options** — basic presets without much customization

If any of these resonate, here are alternatives worth trying.

**Price:** Free  
**Platform:** Web (any device), installable as PWA  
**Best for:** People who want timer + tasks + ambient sounds in one place

Foci is what Forest would be if it took productivity seriously instead of gamification. Timer, task tracking, ambient sounds, daily goals, and streak tracking — all in one window.

- Full task manager with projects and subtasks
- Built-in brown noise, rain, café sounds, and lo-fi music
- Per-task time tracking (know exactly where your hours go)
- Smart Plan: algorithmic task scheduling based on due dates
- Completely free — no ads, no premium tier

**Why you might prefer Forest:**
- No gamification (if growing trees genuinely motivates you)
- No native mobile app (Foci is a PWA)

**Verdict:** The best free alternative if you want a complete focus system, not just a timer.

## 2. Flora — Best Gamification Alternative

**Price:** Free (with in-app purchases)  
**Platform:** iOS, Android  
**Best for:** People who like Forest's gamification but want multiplayer

Flora is the closest alternative to Forest — same tree-growing concept, but with better social features and real-tree planting.

**Key features:**
- Grow trees with focus sessions
- Plant real trees through partner organizations
- Multiplayer rooms to focus with friends
- Habit tracking beyond focus sessions

**Why it's better than Forest:**
- Real tree planting with more transparency
- Better multiplayer features
- Habit tracking (not just focus)

**Why you might prefer Forest:**
- More mature app, larger user base
- Slightly more polished

**Verdict:** Best if you like gamification and want social accountability.

---

## 3. Tide — Best for Ambient Sounds

**Price:** Free (premium $9.99/month)  
**Platform:** iOS, Android  
**Best for:** People who want beautiful ambient soundscapes

Tide combines focus timing with rich, high-quality ambient sounds — ocean waves, forests, rain, and more. It's more about creating a calming environment than gamification.

**Key features:**
- Premium ambient soundscapes
- Focus, sleep, and meditation modes
- Daily content (quotes, recommendations)
- Minimalist design

**Why it's better than Forest:**
- Built-in ambient sounds (Forest has none)
- More relaxing, less stressful approach
- Multiple modes (focus, sleep, etc.)

**Drawbacks:**
- Best sounds require premium ($9.99/month)
- No task tracking
- Limited timer customization

**Verdict:** Best if you want premium ambient sounds and a calming aesthetic.

---

## 4. Be Focused — Best for Apple Users

**Price:** Free (Pro $4.99 removes limits)  
**Platform:** macOS, iOS  
**Best for:** People in the Apple ecosystem who want a native app

Be Focused is a clean, native Pomodoro app for Mac and iPhone with task tracking and basic statistics.

**Key features:**
- Native Mac/iOS apps (not a web app)
- Menu bar timer on Mac
- Task tracking with intervals
- iCloud sync between devices

**Why it's better than Forest:**
- Includes task tracking
- Native Mac app with menu bar timer
- Better timer customization

**Drawbacks:**
- Apple-only (no Windows, Android, or web)
- Free version limited to 1 task list
- No ambient sounds

**Verdict:** Best if you're fully in the Apple ecosystem and want a native app.

---

## 5. Pomofocus — Best Minimalist Option

**Price:** Free (premium removes ads)  
**Platform:** Web  
**Best for:** People who want the simplest possible timer

Pomofocus is a clean, no-frills Pomodoro timer with a basic task list. It loads instantly and does exactly one thing: time your focus sessions.

**Key features:**
- Extremely simple interface
- Basic task list
- Customizable timer durations
- Works in any browser

**Why it's better than Forest:**
- Free (Forest costs $3.99 on iOS)
- Web-based (works anywhere)
- No gamification distractions

**Drawbacks:**
- No ambient sounds
- Ads in free version
- Very basic task tracking
- No offline support

**Verdict:** Best if you want simplicity above all else.
